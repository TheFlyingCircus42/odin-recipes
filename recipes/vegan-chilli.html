Vegan Chilli



A delicious and warming mushroom and vegan chilli that is packed full of goodness! This recipe has been a staple in my kitchen for a long time because it is both tasty and perfect for batch cooking. The recipe calls for three different beans, but you can easily swap them out for your favourites.

Ingredients

  • 1x White Onion (chopped)
  • 3-5 Cloves of Garlic (minced)
  • 1-2 Tablespoons of tomato pure
  • 2x Aubergines (optional)
  • 2x 400g Tins of chopped tomatoes
  • 500g Chopped mushrooms
  • 1-2 Medium carrots (diced into 1cm cubes)
  • 2-3 Sticks of celery (diced into 1cm cubes)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Cumin
  • Paprika
  • Chilli Powder
  • Maple Syrup
  • 1x Stock cube or gel


Recipe

  1. Pre-heat the oven to aprox 180 degrees celcius
  2. Once oven is warmm out the two aubergiens in there on a baking tray (the tray will cath the juice if they split whilst cooking) and set a time for 20 minutes.
  3. Heat some oil in a large pan on a medium heat (I prefer olive oil but any is type is fine).
  4. Cook the onions for aprox 10 minutes (until brown/softened).
  5. Add the carrots and celery and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1-2 more minutes.
  7. Add the spices and toast for 1 minute.
  8. Add a drizzle of maple syrup and stir in.
  9. Add 1-2 tablespoons of tomatoe pure and mix well.
  10. Add the chopped mushrooms and stir well until they are all coated with tomato and spices. COok for 5-10mins.
  11. Add the cans of chopped tomatoes, stir and cook for another 5 minutes. (Tip: put a little bit of water in each can to get the last bits of juice out)
  12. Mix the stock cube with 400-500ml of boiling water and then add to the pot. Stir well and bring to a gentle simmer.
  13. Check on the Aubergines. If the skin is hard (or they have burst) then they can be taken out and left to cool for a a few minutes. If they are not ready, leave for another 5-10 minutes and check again
  14. Open all 3 tins of beans and drain the juices in a collander.
  15. Once drained, add the beans to the pot and stir.
  16. Slice open the aubergines and scrape the soft insides out of the skin into a bowl. Be careful of very hot steam coming from the inside of the aubergienes
  17. Use a hand blender to blitz the aubergine flesh into a smooth paste ans then add to the pot.
  18. Let the whole mix simmer and reduce slightly, stirring occasionally.
  19. If the chilli looks very watery you can reduce further and or add a slurry of cornflour to thicken it. (1-2 tablespoons of cornflour mixed with a little bit of cold water)
  20. Take off the heat and use a hand blender to blitz up some of the mixture. just a few pulses here nd there should be enough to add texture. Be careful not to blitz too much.
  21. Stir well and check the texture is right. It should be mostly wholepieces with smaller chunks.
  22. Let the mixture sit for a while to cool down. Then serve with rice or on a jacket potatoe.
